    Introduction

    The Airbus A380-800 aka superjumbo is a wide-body aircraft manufactured by Airbus. It is the world's largest passenger airliner challenging the Boeing 747. On 19 December 2000, Airbus launched the $10.7 billion A380 programme. The first delivery was made on 15 October 2007 to Singapore Airlines which entered service on 25th October 2007.













    Capacity Comparison With Other Airliners

    The A380-800 has the most capacity in an airliner. It has a typical seating of 525 passengers, with a maximum certified capacity of 853 passengers.

    End Of An Era

    As of October 2021, Airbus has received 251 firm orders, of which 248 had been delivered. Airbus target was to sell 700 of the A380-800 in total. However, Airbus was forced to cease production due to lack of demand. Some of the reasons were: unfilled seats were unprofitable, rising prices of fuel, environmental inpact, airlines opting for smaller, but more efficient planes produced by both Boeing & Airbus .

    Some airlines were more interested in Boeing's new family of 777s which offers fewer seats, but same range, more payload & 2 fewer engines .

    Even though the A380-800 production is soon to cease, it would be unlikely for the airliner to disappear from the skies any time soon. However, this airliner will forever be remembered for its engineering marvel.
