    Introduction

    The Airbus A380 aka superjumbo, is a wide-body aircraft manufactured by Airbus. It is the world's largest passenger airliner. Airbus's plan was to challenge the Boeing 747 which was dominating the long haul market. Studies have been carried out in 1988 and the project was announced in 1990. On 19 December 2000, Airbus launched the $10.7 billion A380 programme. The first delivery was made on 15 October 2007 to Singapore Airlines. In 2019, Airbus announced that the A380 production would end in 2021 as its $25 billion investment can't be recouped and demand for the airliner was dropping.

    End Of An Era

    As of October 2021, Airbus has received 251 firm orders, of which 248 had been delivered. Airbus's target was to sell 700 of the A380-800 in total. However, Airbus was forced to cease production of the A380-800 due to lack of demand. Even though the A380-800 was popular with passengers, most airlines view it as inefficient. Some of the reasons were: unfilled seats were unprofitable, rising prices of fuel, environmental impact, airlines opting for smaller, but more efficient planes produced by both Boeing and Airbus.

    Some airlines were interested in Boeing's new family of 777s which deliveries would start in 2020. It offers fewer seats, but the same range as the A380-800, more payload and 2 fewer engines.

    Even though the A380-800 production is soon to cease, it would be unlikely for the airliner to disappear from the skies any time soon. However, this airliner will be remembered for its engineering marvel.
